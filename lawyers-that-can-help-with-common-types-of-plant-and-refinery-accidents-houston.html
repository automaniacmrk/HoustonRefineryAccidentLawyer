Lawyers that can help with Common Types Of Plant And Refinery Accidents Houston

Due to heavy machinery and hazardous substances, as well as the complex tools that are used in an industrial plant or refinery, it is important to follow the safety regulations set forth by the federal authorities and the site supervisors. The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency states that the vast majority of plant and refinery accidents are due to a lack of safety regulations, poor equipment design, inadequate maintenance, or failure to respond to warnings. Not wearing the right safety protections, inadequately training employees, or choosing to ignore an environmental hazard are all examples of how failing to follow safety guidelines could result in a refinery or oil plant accident. Other dangers at plants and refineries include:

  • Slips and Falls
  • Falling objects from great heights
  • The careless use of heavy machinery
  • Products or tools that are defective
  • Explosions and fires
  • Chemical burns and inhalation of toxic fumes
  • Transporting or storing hazardous material in an improper manner

If these conditions were due to negligence by another party, an injured worker at a plant or refinery can begin their claim for compensation with one of our Houston lawyers.

If Your Employer At A Refinery Plant Carries Workers' Compensation

You can claim benefits if your employer has workers' comp insurance. You may qualify for the following benefits if you sustain an injury on the job:

  • Medical benefits for the explosion-related injuries or illness
  • Partially replacing lost wages with income benefits
  • If a loved one has passed away, funeral and death benefits are available

These benefits are often not available to workers because employers and their insurance companies don't always make it simple for them. If your employer provides them, you may be entitled. However, it can sometimes prove difficult to file for them.

Our Houston refinery explosion lawyers can guide you through the claim filing process, helping to ensure that all necessary information is included and your initial claim is accepted. You can appeal a denied benefits claim.
