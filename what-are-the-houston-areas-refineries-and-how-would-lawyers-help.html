What are the Houston Area's Refineries and how would Lawyers help?

Ten major oil refineries are located in the Houston metropolitan area. These refineries process more than half of Texas' oil production, according to the Greater Houston Partnership. More than one-fourth the oil produced in America is processed by refineries located in Texas' other regions, such as Port Arthur, Galveston Corpus Christi Baytown and Beaumont.

This means that thousands of Texas workers are working in the refineries. The Houston Chronicle reported in 2020 that Texas as a whole boasts nearly 150,000 workers in the oil and gas industry.

Refineries play an important role in the economy of Houston and all of Texas. As a result, it is important for oil and gas workers to know their rights if they are injured in an accident or explosion on the job.

What causes construction and oil refinery accidents?

OSHA states that there are more fatal incidents at refineries than in the three other highest-ranking industries. OSHA reports that 36 refinery deaths were due to a gaseous leakage between 1992 and 2007. From these refinery accidents, 52 employee deaths 250 injuries stemmed from the incidents. It would stand to reason then, that every year an average of three people die and 16 are injured in the construction and maintenance of oil refineries. However, these numbers are deceivingly low.

In an attempt to make their plants seem safe, oil refineries hire as many contractors as possible. When reporting injuries and deaths, they do not have to "claim" contractors as they are not employees of the petroleum companies.

These are the most common causes of accidents in construction and accidents at oil refineries:

  • Explosions
  • Feuers
  • Equipment failure
  • Exposure to Chemicals
